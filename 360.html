Xbox 360 Controller

Launched as the Xbox 360's official Controller in 2005 alongside the system, the Xbox 360 controller was a fairly big step up from the Original Xbox's controller (also known as 'the Duke'). Unlike its predecessor, the Xbox 360 Controller came in both wired and wireless configurations. The controller came in a variety of colours, including: white, black, dark blue, light blue, pink, white S and Black S. The wireless variations of the controller were powered by 2 AA batteries, or a rechargeable battery pack.

The controller featured an A, B, X, and Y button, 2 triggers, 2 shoulder buttons, a D-Pad, 2 Analog Sticks which were clickable, a start button, and a select button.

Another big change of the controller came in the form of its form-factor. The Xbox 360 Controller was quite a significant amount smaller than 'The Duke.'

It also featured a 2.5mm TRS connector which allowed users to have the option to use voice connectivity.
